Translated and Subtitled Videos

Translations are part of my life. Even though I decided to stop pursuing this as a career path, I don't think I'll ever stop translating because I know this is important. As we say in Ted Translators Program, we are "connecting people to extraordinary ideas." I know that humans connect better with an idea when it's delivered in their mother tong so since I have the ability to transmit the speaker's feelings and beliefs across the language barrier, I consider as my responsibility to do it so other people can learn something new, and to access information which otherwise they couldn't.

Here are only three of the TED Talks I translated and developed subtitles. To see the rest of my work, visit my TED Translator's Profile.

Social psychologist Thomas Curran explores how the pressure to be perfect -- in our social media feeds, in school, at work -- is driving a rise in mental illness, especially among young people. Learn more about the causes of this phenomenon and how we can create a culture that celebrates the joys of imperfection.

Imagine a distant future when humans reach beyond Earth, forge cities on planets thousands of light-years away, and maintain a galactic web of trade and transport. What would it take to make that leap? And where would we get enough energy to power that civilization? Fabio Pacucci explores the possibility of harnessing energy from a black hole to power a galactic civilization. [Directed by TOTEM Studio, narrated by Addison Anderson, music by Stephen LaRosa].

Of all the mundane yet astonishing marvels of human ingenuity, knowing what it takes to heat a room to a comfortable temperature is TED Fellow Kathy Hannun's favorite. She takes us on a journey across the planet and under the sea to emphasize the dangers of modern heating, and offers a safer, planet-friendly alternative that taps into the geothermal energy right below our feet.
