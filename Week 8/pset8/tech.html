For my entire life, I've been good at lots of things and study areas. However, instead of being able to decide what to be when I grow up, it only confused me because some said that I should do something with which I'm good. So, over the years, my goals changed from Civil Engineering to Architecture to Business Management and Recruiting to Theoretical Physicist to Physics' Tenure Professor.

When I was about to apply for a scholarship for a bachelor's degree in Physics, I came across an article about web development and got a little curious. After some research, I felt that I should take a closer look at it. That's when I found Harvard's CS50 course and, after the first code I wrote in C, I knew that's what I suppose to do, and a few weeks later, I decided to be a Developer.

How do I know that this is what I want to do? I'm so glad you asked. If you take a look at the professions I chose over the years, you'll understand:

Profession What I like about it
Architecture/Civil Engineering Create and design, math, increase people's quality of life
Business Management/Recruiting Leadership, help people show their best
Theoretical Physicist/Physics' Tenure Professor Math, create things, teaching, always learning something new

What about being a Developer? It encapsulates everything that I like from all of those professions.

Visit my repository on GitHub to see my Labs and Problem Set's Solutions.

I had a brief Graphic Designing phase when I was helping people close to me to take off their newly opened business. Despite my want of previous understanding of the subject, I learned surprisingly fast how to edit photos and vectors and, even though I didn't choose this as a career, I know that these abilities will only grow and won't become wasted, especially when I'm developing websites.
